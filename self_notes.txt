Model_I — Synthetic Gaussian PSF

Sharpest, cleanest images of all four
PSF is a perfect mathematical Gaussian (not real)
Low, uniform noise
Best for: initial ML training, understanding pure lensing features without instrument effects
Your images above show this — very clean ring edges, deep black background

Model_II — Euclid VIS

Slightly blurrier than Model_I (PSF FWHM 0.18″ vs 0.12″)
More realistic noise pattern from the Euclid instrument
Larger pixel scale (0.1″/px) so rings look slightly softer
Best for: preparing models for real Euclid survey data (launching now)

Model_III — HST ACS/WFC

Finest pixel scale (0.05″/px) — twice the resolution of Euclid
Sharpest real-telescope PSF (0.09″) since Hubble is above the atmosphere
Sub-halo distortions and arc fine structure are most visible
Best for: detecting faint substructure, highest scientific precision
Your earlier CDM/axion images were Model_III — notice how crisp the arcs were

Model_IV — Euclid VIS (wide-z variant)

Identical instrument to Model_II
Only difference is wider redshift priors — lens can be at z=0.2–0.9, source up to z=3.0
Produces more diverse Einstein ring sizes across the dataset
Best for: training robust models that generalise across a wider range of lens configurations
